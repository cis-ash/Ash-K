Problem Space

Problem Space:

Create a chain of one-way connections that connect dynamically updated designer inputs and the environment to expressive actions and visual representations of the moving character visible to the player.

Applications:

Games where the NPC and player creatures, their behavior, emotions, movements are the focus of the experience. Games where NPC or player creatures must move through complex terrain/express various emotions through movement/have a lot of modifiers affecting behavior or animations that would be impossible to animate by hand due to the combined number of needed variations

Relevance and Potential to Demonstrate Expertise

Topic Relevance:

This approach to animations is often underexplored in indie spaces, and when it is explored it usually lacks the emotional element that made commonly cited inspirations like spore and rain world so special. Animation is a very labor-intensive task, so making certain parts of the game procedurally animated allows the characters to show a wider array of inner variables without the task becoming for the animators. Making the characters animated in such a manner also frees up animators to spend more time on other animations in the game as well as hero animations which are more likely to be remembered by the player than 50 different walk loops.

Potential to Demonstrate Expertise

If you want to have expressive characters that respond to their environment in your game, you can either get a bunch of animators to work long hours or ask me to make you some creatures in a week and let your animators do something that will be of more value per hour of work for the player.

Jiggly follow-through and IK

 

Prototype only half finished, thoughts may change in the future:

  • Procedural jiggly bits add a whole lot of flavor and life to a creature while taking next to no effort

  • IK is really easy for single knee legs with equal sized bones

  • Leg IK can be skinned with a Bezier curve to make a neck or a noodle style appendage

  • Line2D nodes make creation of creatures like this exceptionally easy but lack texture, might be a good idea to use the built in bones and skeleton based polygon deformations to make legs a bendy sprite instead (how are knees gonna be handled?)

  • There is a certain balance between control over character and how natural it looks. Obviously player controlled fellas will need it cranked one way to be responsive and NPCs need it cranked the other for telegraphing and believability

    • this does not apply to bits adding visual flair, they can be as floppy and have as much follow-through as needed. As long as the center of mass is responsive to inputs it feels good
